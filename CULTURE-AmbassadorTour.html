local_floristSU Campus Ambassador Tour

groups SUSG COMMITTEE ON STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS

10/11/2023

WHAT?

The SU Campus Ambassador Tour, assigned as an alternative to GE10 classes, unfolded as an engaging and informative event organized by the SUSG Committee on Student Organization. The tour was led by two representatives who navigated students through various campus locations, sharing intriguing facts and stories along the way. An example included the explanation that the orange doors on the second floor of the CBA building serve as evacuation areas during emergencies. Another fascinating tidbit was the book held by the Hibbard statue in Hibbard Hall being "The First Quarter by Hibbard." This unique experience offered a blend of historical insights and fun facts about the campus.

SO WHAT?

Participating in the SU Campus Ambassador Tour provided an enriching and enjoyable experience. The event not only served as a creative alternative to regular classes but also offered a deeper understanding of the history and unique features of Silliman University. Learning about the significance behind campus elements, like the orange doors and the book held by the Hibbard statue, added layers of appreciation for the alma mater. The engaging format of the tour made it a memorable opportunity to connect with the university's rich history.


NOW WHAT?

The Campus Ambassador Tour has left a lasting impact, inspiring a deeper connection with the university's history and architecture. The newfound knowledge about campus features and their significance enhances the overall sense of belonging to Silliman University. Moving forward, I am motivated to actively explore and appreciate the unique elements and history that contribute to the rich tapestry of the alma mater.



Because of the SU Campus Ambassador Tour, I am motivated to embrace a deeper connection with the history and unique features of Silliman University. The event has inspired me to actively seek out and appreciate the distinctive elements that contribute to the university's identity. This newfound knowledge enhances my sense of pride and connection with my alma mater, fostering a greater appreciation for the rich history embedded in the campus.
