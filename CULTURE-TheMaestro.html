local_floristRYAN CAYABYAB : THE MAESTRO RETURNS

groups "SU CULTURE AND ARTS COUNCIL"

11/25/2023

WHAT?

"Ryan Cayabyab: The Maestro Returns" unfolded as a captivating event inside Claire Isabel McGill Luce Auditorium. This mini-concert featured Mr. Ryan Cayabyab and the "Ryan Cayabyab Singers (RCS)." Unlike a traditional performance, this event showcased tributes to songs from artists like Michael Jackson, Carpenters, The Greatest Showman, and more. The unique twist involved the RCS dancing while singing, with Mr. Cayabyab skillfully playing the piano.

SO WHAT?

The concert, offering a departure from Mr. Cayabyab's usual repertoire, provided an interesting and entertaining experience. While some Baby Boomers recognized certain songs, many attendees, including myself, were introduced to unfamiliar tunes. Despite the diverse familiarity with the songs, the performance, featuring energetic singing and dancing by the RCS, along with Mr. Cayabyab's piano mastery, created an engaging and enjoyable atmosphere. The event served as an eye-opener to a variety of music genres, expanding my musical horizon.


NOW WHAT?

"Ryan Cayabyab: The Maestro Returns" has inspired a newfound appreciation for diverse musical genres. The experience of discovering and enjoying songs outside my usual preferences has motivated me to explore more eclectic musical offerings. Moving forward, I am inclined to be more open to different musical styles, recognizing the enriching experience that comes from embracing a broader range of artistic expressions.



Because of "Ryan Cayabyab: The Maestro Returns," I am inspired to broaden my musical preferences and explore diverse genres. The concert, with its unique tributes and energetic performances, has opened my eyes to new and unfamiliar music. Moving forward, I am motivated to embrace a more eclectic musical taste, appreciating the richness and diversity that different genres bring to the artistic landscape.
